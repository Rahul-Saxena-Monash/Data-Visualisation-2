Ocean Acidification

pH Deviation Map

This map shows the deviation of pH in the ocean surrounding Australia from the global average in 2013.

The pH deviation is measured using the pH scale, where a deviation of 0 is the global average. The positive deviations (more basic) are in red, and the negative deviations (more acidic) are in blue.

Ocean Acidification Time Series

This section illustrates the changes in key variables related to ocean acidification over time using a line chart. Use the dropdown menu to select different variables:

  • pH: Measures acidity. A decreasing pH indicates increasing acidity, which can harm marine organisms, especially those with calcium carbonate shells or skeletons.
  • Sea Surface Temperature (SST): Rising temperatures can accelerate chemical reactions and reduce the ocean's capacity to absorb CO2, exacerbating acidification.
  • Sea Surface Salinity (SSS): Changes in salinity can affect the ocean's ability to absorb CO2 and impact marine ecosystems.
  • Aragonite Saturation State (OMEGA_A): A decreasing value indicates it's harder for marine organisms to form aragonite shells, crucial for many species' survival.
  • Calcite Saturation State (OMEGA_C): Similar to aragonite, a lower value makes it more difficult for organisms to form calcite shells, affecting various marine species.
